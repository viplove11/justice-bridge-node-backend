Example 1:
In the Supreme Court of India, the appellant challenged the High Court judgment convicting him under Section 302 IPC. The Court examined eyewitness testimony, medical evidence, and the timing of the FIR. It held that the prosecution proved guilt beyond reasonable doubt and upheld the conviction and life sentence.

Example 2:
Before the Delhi High Court, a writ petition challenged the cancellation of a government tender on grounds of arbitrariness and violation of natural justice. The Court found that the authority failed to provide reasons and set aside the cancellation, directing a fresh decision after hearing the petitioner.

Example 3:
The Bombay High Court considered a criminal appeal in a dowry death case under Section 304B IPC. The Court noted that the death occurred within seven years of marriage and there was evidence of dowry harassment. It affirmed the presumption under Section 113B of the Evidence Act and upheld the conviction.

Example 4:
In the Supreme Court of India, the accused sought bail in a POCSO case. The Court assessed the seriousness of the allegations, the victim’s statement, and the stage of trial. It denied bail, observing that the statutory presumption and the need to protect the victim outweighed the grounds raised.

Example 5:
The Karnataka High Court reviewed a civil second appeal regarding a property partition dispute. After examining the family genealogy, title documents, and prior decrees, the Court found no substantial question of law and dismissed the appeal, confirming the partition decree.
